About

I am currently a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellow at Politecnico di Torino, leading the research project VIBRATIONCLEAR, which aims to protect buildings against earthquakes through the use of inerter-based vibrating barriers. I hold a BEng (2009–2013) and an MSc (2014–2015) in Civil and Structural Engineering, and completed my PhD at the University of Sheffield in 2021. Prior to my current position, I was a JSPS Postdoctoral Fellow at Tohoku University, Japan (2023-2025). I also held a postdoctoral research fellowship at ETH Zürich, funded by the Swiss Government Excellence Scholarship (2022-2023), and served as a postdoctoral researcher at Dong-A University, South Korea (2022). My research focuses on structural dynamics and earthquake engineering. During my PhD studies, I conducted the world’s first shake-table experiment on a multi-storey structure equipped with an inerter-based damper system, carried out at the Structural Dynamics Laboratory for Verification and Validation (LVV) at the University of Sheffield. Since 2018, I have published extensively in reputable journals and conferences. One of my papers was recognized as a “Top Cited Article” in Earthquake Engineering and Structural Dynamics (EESD), published by Wiley.

Interests: Structural Dynamics, vibration control, seismic isolation, soil–structure interaction, finite elements, and AI in vibration engineering.
